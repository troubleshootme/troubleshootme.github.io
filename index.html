With over 6,000 loads available, equipment availability is becoming a critical factor, and the premium for reliable reefer capacity is re-asserting itself, limiting broker negotiation leverage.
Flatbeds The flatbed market remains exceptionally favorable for brokers, showing no signs of tightening. Despite a massive volume of nearly 27,000 available loads, ample capacity continues to suppress pricing. Current paid rates are averaging just $2.15/mile, well below the average posted rate of $2.26/mile and significantly under week-old benchmark data. This wide gap confirms a highly competitive environment where carriers are aggressively bidding on freight. This gives brokers substantial leverage to negotiate favorable rates for customers, as demand from construction and industrial sectors is not sufficient to absorb the current equipment surplus.
Diesel Price $3.727/gal - The national diesel average has seen a minor dip to $3.727/gallon. However, market reports indicate a price increase is expected early this week, which will increase carrier operating costs and likely lead to firmer rate negotiations on fuel-intensive lanes.
OTRI Trends Tender rejections are expected to diverge by equipment type. Van and reefer rejections are likely to rise modestly as firming spot rates make contract freight less attractive for carriers. Conversely, flatbed rejections will remain low, as abundant capacity and soft rates incentivize carriers to accept nearly all tendered loads to maintain utilization. Localized rejection spikes are possible in areas of Kentucky and Illinois affected by flash flooding.
