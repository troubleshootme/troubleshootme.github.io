üìä Top-Line Summary

The national freight market is defined by significant regional volatility driven by severe weather events, creating both risk and opportunity for brokers. Widespread flooding and thunderstorms across the Southeast, particularly Georgia, are causing major operational disruptions, tightening capacity, and creating urgent spot freight demand. Concurrently, an intense heatwave continues to plague the Southwest, severely constraining reefer availability and elevating rates. Nationally, spot market data indicates carriers are gaining leverage, with paid rates for van and reefer equipment consistently exceeding posted rates, signaling a firming market where low bids are increasingly rejected. Brokers must focus on navigating weather-related disruptions and capitalizing on the resulting rate premiums and capacity imbalances.
