Vans The van market remains a pocket of stability, with available loads holding steady around 13,500. The average paid rate is $1.83/mile, almost perfectly aligned with the average posted rate of $1.84/mile. This razor-thin gap indicates a highly balanced market where capacity is meeting demand almost exactly, leaving little room for negotiation on either side. For brokers, this translates to predictable but tight margins, where operational efficiency and securing reliable capacity are key, as rate volatility is minimal.
Reefers Extreme market tension defines the reefer sector, as paid rates have surged to an average of $2.53/mile, a staggering 20 cents above the posted average of $2.33/mile. This significant rate inversion, coupled with a drop in available loads to just over 4,900, is a direct result of the expansive heatwave across the West and Southwest. The intense heat is straining equipment and driver availability, creating urgent demand for temperature-controlled capacity. This carrier-favorable environment allows brokers with strong reefer carrier relationships to secure premium rates and capitalize on the severe capacity crunch.
Flatbeds The flatbed market is currently the most significant opportunity zone for freight brokers. While load availability is robust at over 21,000, the average paid rate has fallen to $1.81/mile, creating a massive 40-cent gap below the average posted rate of $2.21/mile. This spread has more than doubled since Friday, signaling a substantial surplus of capacity relative to current demand. This dynamic provides brokers with exceptional negotiating leverage, allowing for significantly increased margins on spot freight and favorable terms on contracted loads.
Diesel Price $3.686/gal - Diesel prices have slightly decreased, offering minor relief to carriers. However, the overall cost remains elevated, ensuring that fuel efficiency and deadhead miles continue to be critical factors in carrier negotiations and load acceptance.
