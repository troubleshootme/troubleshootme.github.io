This equilibrium, slightly below week-ago averages of $2.31/mile, is sustained by peak produce seasons in the Southeast and West Coast. While capacity is meeting demand, it comes at a premium, leaving little room for negotiation on headhauls. The key for brokers is identifying backhaul opportunities and positioning carriers for lucrative outbound loads from key agricultural zones. The market is predictable but expensive, rewarding brokers with strong carrier relationships.
Flatbeds Significant opportunity has emerged in the flatbed sector. Despite a massive volume of over 35,800 available loads, real-time paid rates have fallen to $2.27/mile, well below week-ago averages of $2.55/mile. This sharp drop indicates that a surge in available capacity is giving brokers significant buying power. The gap between the posted rate ($2.25/mile) and the paid rate ($2.27/mile) is minimal, but the overall rate decline is the key takeaway. Brokers should aggressively target flatbed freight, as market conditions are highly favorable for securing capacity at competitive rates for construction and industrial freight.
Diesel Price $3.73/gal - The national average diesel price has ticked up slightly to $3.73/gallon. This minor increase adds to carrier operating costs, reinforcing the financial pressures noted in recent earnings reports. While not a primary driver of line-haul rates, it's a valid talking point in negotiations, as carriers are focused on covering all variable expenses.
OTRI Trends Tender rejections are likely to become more targeted. With flatbed rates softening, expect lower rejection rates in that segment as carriers accept loads to maintain utilization. Van and reefer rejections will remain concentrated in high-demand markets like the Southeast and California, where spot rates offer a significant premium over contract rates. Nationally, the trend is stabilizing, but regional hot spots will persist.
