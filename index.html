üìä Top-Line Summary

The national freight market is currently a tale of two extremes, creating significant arbitrage opportunities for agile brokers. In the Southeast, widespread flood warnings across Georgia and Alabama are actively disrupting supply chains, tightening capacity, and creating a surge in demand for urgent spot freight. Conversely, an oppressive heatwave in the Southwest is straining reefer equipment and driver availability. National spot data reveals a critical divergence: reefer rates are being driven up by these pressures with paid rates exceeding posted rates, while van and flatbed markets are showing signs of softening, with paid rates now falling below posted averages. This creates a complex but opportunity-rich environment where regional expertise and strategic sourcing are paramount.

This provides brokers with negotiation leverage and the ability to secure trucks at or below initial asking prices, particularly in regions unaffected by major weather disruptions.
Diesel Price $3.74/gal - The national average diesel price holds firm at $3.74/gallon. While stable, this elevated price continues to exert pressure on carrier operating costs, making them sensitive to rate negotiations and reinforcing the need for accurate fuel surcharge calculations in all quotes.
OTRI Trends Tender rejection trends are diverging significantly by equipment type and region. Reefer rejections are highest and climbing, particularly for loads originating in or passing through the heat-affected Southwest (AZ, CA) and flood-threatened Southeast (GA, AL). Conversely, the national van and flatbed segments are likely seeing lower rejection rates as indicated by paid rates falling below posted rates, suggesting carriers are more willing to accept loads at initial tender.
