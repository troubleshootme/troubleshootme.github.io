⛽ Fuel strategy: how to sell and buy in a $4.86 diesel market

  • Separate the quote into three pieces.
    • Linehaul
    • Fuel surcharge (FSC)
    • Risk premium
  • Why this matters
    • Customers resist a single higher number.
    • They accept a structured explanation:
    • base linehaul
    • fuel reality
    • weather or operational risk
  • What to stop doing today
    • Stop bundling everything into one vague rate
    • Stop honoring stale quote windows
    • Stop treating long-haul and short-haul freight as if fuel hits them equally
  • What to push today
    • Shorter quote validity
    • Lane-specific fuel updates
    • Short-haul and relay-friendly solutions
    • Consolidation where service requirements allow
  • Carrier-side implication
    • At $4.86/gallon, carriers are much more likely to reject:
    • cheap long-haul
    • deadhead-heavy freight
    • appointment-sensitive freight without detention protection
    • uncertain unloading environments
