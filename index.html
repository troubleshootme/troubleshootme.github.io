While nearly 7,000 loads are visible on spot boards, securing reliable reefer capacity for produce will command a premium this week.
Flatbeds The flatbed market has undergone a significant reversal, shifting from a broker-favorable environment to a firming market. Paid rates have surged to $2.25/mile, closing the gap with the average posted rate of $2.27/mile. This rapid tightening, observed across nearly 36,000 available loads, suggests that the previous surplus of capacity has been absorbed by renewed industrial and construction activity. The narrative of a soft flatbed market is no longer valid; brokers must now contend with rising carrier expectations and less negotiation leverage. This upward trend is a critical shift from last week's conditions.

OTRI Trends Tender rejections for flatbed are likely to increase this week as rising spot rates make contract freight less appealing for carriers. Van and reefer rejection rates are expected to remain stable but elevated, reflecting a balanced but tight market where carriers have viable spot market alternatives. Expect localized rejection spikes in produce-heavy regions and near major ports.
