üìà National Data Dashboard

Vans A subtle but important shift is occurring in the van market. After a period of softening, real-time paid rates ($1.93/mile) have now edged above average posted rates ($1.89/mile), indicating that carriers are regaining some pricing power on the most desirable loads. While rates remain below the benchmarks from a week ago, this reversal suggests capacity is tightening slightly or that demand for the 15,600+ available loads is solidifying. Driver availability appears to be meeting general demand, but brokers may find less room for negotiation than in previous days and should anticipate carriers holding firm on rates for well-positioned equipment.
Reefers The reefer market is characterized by stability, a sign that mid-summer produce seasons are hitting a predictable, sustained rhythm rather than a volatile peak. Real-time paid rates are holding steady and are identical to average posted rates ($2.30/mile), which also aligns closely with week-old market averages. This equilibrium suggests that temperature-controlled capacity is well-matched to current demand from the 6,000+ available loads. For brokers, this translates to predictable pricing and reliable capacity, reducing risk on reefer lanes, though opportunities for significant rate negotiation are limited.
Flatbeds The flatbed sector remains a deeply favorable market for brokers, with softness accelerating. There is a significant spread between average posted rates ($2.26/mile) and what is actually being paid to carriers ($2.15/mile), highlighting intense competition for freight among carriers. This is further evidenced by the sharp drop from week-old rate benchmarks, which were closer to $2.55/mile. Despite a high volume of nearly 27,000 available loads, an oversupply of equipment is suppressing rates, providing brokers with substantial negotiation leverage and the ability to secure capacity at a discount.
Diesel Price $3.727/gal - The national average holds just under $3.73/gallon, but market reports indicate prices are expected to rise this week. Brokers should factor a potential increase in fuel surcharges into their quoting and carrier negotiations for the coming days.
OTRI Trends Tender rejection trends are diverging. Van rejections may level off or see a slight uptick as carriers regain minor pricing power. Flatbed rejections will remain exceptionally low as carriers are eager to accept any load in a soft market. Reefer rejections are expected to stay stable, mirroring the market's overall equilibrium. Localized rejection spikes are likely in areas affected by flash flooding in the Appalachians and Southwest.
