üìä Top-Line Summary

The freight market presents a highly segmented landscape today. The flatbed sector continues to show exceptional strength with over 36,000 available loads, though real-time paid rates are tracking just below posted averages, indicating capacity is stretching to meet the historic demand. In contrast, the van market remains soft and stable, with paid rates matching posted rates, offering a favorable environment for shippers. Reefer capacity is firm, driven by seasonal produce, commanding slight premiums on the spot market. Key external factors include rising cargo theft incidents, which necessitates heightened security diligence, and ongoing legislative discussions in Washington regarding truck parking and potential price regulations that could influence carrier relations and customer pricing strategies long-term.

üìà National Data Dashboard

Vans The van market remains stable and relatively soft, with real-time paid rates holding steady at $1.85/mile, matching the average posted rate across more than 17,500 available loads. This equilibrium suggests that while summer retail and CPG freight provides consistent demand, overall capacity is ample and well-positioned to absorb current volumes, keeping rates in check. Driver availability for standard van freight is not a major constraint, which prevents any significant upward rate pressure. This environment, showing a slight softening from week-old benchmark data, presents opportunities for brokers to secure competitive pricing for shippers.
Reefers Reefer markets are firm as seasonal demand continues to drive competition for temperature-controlled equipment. With over 7,200 loads available, the average paid rate of $2.21/mile is ticking just above the posted average of $2.20, signaling that carriers hold slight pricing power. This premium is a direct result of tight capacity in key agricultural regions like the Southeast and West Coast, where produce harvests are in full swing. While the market isn't seeing runaway inflation, securing reliable reefer equipment requires paying at or near asking prices, as carriers can be selective with loads.
Flatbeds The flatbed segment is experiencing a period of massive demand, with real-time load boards showing over 36,000 available loads. Despite this immense volume from construction and industrial sectors, capacity is responding effectively, with the average paid rate of $2.27/mile settling just a penny below the posted average of $2.28. This indicates that while the market is incredibly active, it is not entirely one-sided; there is enough equipment in the network to prevent significant rate spikes. However, the sheer number of available loads means that carrier optionality is high, and securing capacity requires efficiency and strong relationships.
Diesel Price $3.73/gal - The national average diesel price remains stable, providing a degree of predictability for fuel surcharges and carrier operating costs. This stability helps in quoting but does not alleviate the overall high cost of fuel, which continues to be a primary factor in carrier profitability.
OTRI Trends Tender rejections are highest in the reefer sector, where strong spot market rates incentivize carriers to turn down contracted freight. Flatbed rejections are moderate; despite high volumes, carriers are accepting loads as capacity meets demand. Van tender rejections remain low, consistent with a soft market where carriers are more likely to accept tendered loads due to fewer high-paying alternatives.
