üìä Top-Line Summary

The national freight market presents a mixed signal today. While total load volumes continue to show an upward trend across most equipment types, spot rates are reportedly softening, creating a challenging environment for carriers and a nuanced opportunity for brokers. A severe and widespread heatwave across the Southeast and Midwest is the dominant operational factor, severely constraining reefer capacity and creating service risks. This weather-induced pressure, especially on temperature-controlled freight, contrasts with the broader market's slight rate decline. A major acquisition in the freight technology space further signals ongoing consolidation and a shift in the competitive landscape of digital load board platforms.

This premium is driven by a massive heatwave across the Southeast and Midwest, which increases equipment stress, raises fuel consumption for cooling, and heightens demand for temperature-controlled transport. With available loads increasing to 11,638, the combination of rising demand and extreme weather is tightening capacity significantly. Brokers must be prepared for higher rates and potential service delays, but this also creates opportunities to service urgent, high-margin freight for customers with critical temperature-sensitive shipments.
Flatbeds The flatbed sector remains the most active by volume, with 44,825 available loads. However, the market shows signs of equilibrium or slight softening, as the average paid rate ($2.26/mile) is just under the average posted rate ($2.27/mile). This indicates that while demand from construction and industrial sectors is strong and increasing, capacity is largely keeping pace. This provides a stable environment for brokers to move high volumes of freight without the extreme rate volatility seen in the reefer sector, though margins may be tighter.
Diesel Price $3.734/gal - The national average diesel price has seen a slight decrease to $3.734/gallon after a period of increases. While minor, this dip offers a small measure of cost relief for carriers, though fuel remains a significant operational expense that continues to prop up baseline rates.
OTRI Trends Tender rejections for reefer loads are expected to be high and likely to increase, particularly in the heat-affected Southeast and Midwest. Carriers will be rejecting more loads due to equipment strain and the higher risk/cost of operation. Van and flatbed rejections may remain stable, but regional spikes are possible where extreme weather disrupts operations.
