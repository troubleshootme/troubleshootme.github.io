üìä Top-Line Summary

The national freight market shows signs of firming to start the week, with a slight rebound in load volumes and a notable shift in rate dynamics where paid rates are now exceeding posted rates for vans and reefers. This indicates carriers are gaining negotiation leverage. However, the market is dominated by two major weather systems creating significant regional volatility. Extreme heat across the Southwest is constricting reefer capacity and pushing rates higher on critical lanes. Simultaneously, widespread flood watches in the Southeast are creating imminent disruption, tightening capacity and generating urgent spot freight opportunities. Brokers should focus on these volatile regions to capitalize on weather-driven rate premiums and capacity imbalances.
