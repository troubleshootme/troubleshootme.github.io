üìä Top-Line Summary

The national spot market is showing signs of life, with increasing load volumes across all major equipment types week-over-week. üìà National Data Dashboard

Vans The dry van market is experiencing a surge in volume, with loads moved today significantly outpacing currently available loads, indicating a healthy velocity. Despite this, capacity remains relatively loose, which is keeping a lid on rates, with the average paid rate at $1.88/mile. While total load availability has been trending upward over the past week, the abundance of available carriers is preventing significant rate inflation. Brokers should focus on the spread between posted and paid rates, as the $0.07 difference suggests negotiation room and an opportunity to capture margin on well-positioned loads.
Reefers The reefer market is tightening, driven by seasonal produce demand and compounded by extreme heat across the southern U.S. Paid rates are strong at $2.33/mile, a significant $0.10 premium over posted rates, signaling that shippers are willing to pay for reliable temperature-controlled capacity. Available loads are increasing, and with a high number of loads already moved, equipment is in high demand. This environment creates significant opportunity for brokers, particularly on lanes moving out of agricultural and heat-affected regions where capacity is at a premium.
Flatbeds The flatbed sector is robust, with the highest volume of available loads and moved loads among all equipment types. Paid rates are averaging $2.24/mile, slightly below the posted average, suggesting some rate competition exists despite strong demand from construction and industrial sectors. The consistent increase in flatbed loads over the past five days points to sustained momentum. Brokers can capitalize on this by securing capacity for multi-stop or project-based freight, as carriers are actively seeking loads in a high-volume market.
Diesel Price $3.74/gal - The national average diesel price of $3.74/gallon remains a critical factor for carrier operating costs. While relatively stable, this price point continues to pressure carrier margins, making fuel surcharge accuracy paramount in negotiations. Brokers should leverage this in discussions, as carriers will be highly sensitive to deadhead miles and fuel efficiency.
OTRI Trends While specific tender rejection data is not available, the increasing load volumes and the premium between paid and posted rates in the reefer market suggest that rejection rates are likely rising in that sector. As carriers gain more options in a busier spot market, they are more inclined to reject less favorable contracted freight. This trend increases spot market freight availability, creating opportunities for brokers to service overflow from shippers.
