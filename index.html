üìä Top-Line Summary

The national freight market is showing signs of cooling, with spot rates for van and flatbed dipping despite increasing load volumes. This suggests a slight loosening of capacity, presenting a strategic shift from yesterday's tightening conditions. However, this trend is sharply contrasted by the reefer market, where an intensifying heatwave across the Southeast and Southwest is creating critical capacity shortages and driving rates upward. Brokers face a bifurcated market: an opportunity for margin on general freight and a significant challenge securing temperature-controlled capacity. Ongoing tariff discussions and regulatory changes add another layer of complexity, requiring vigilance on international freight movements.

Diesel Price $3.74/gal - The national average diesel price is stable at $3.74/gallon. This predictability is a minor relief for carriers, but the sustained high cost continues to impact operating margins, making fuel surcharge accuracy a critical component of negotiations.

Conversely, van and flatbed rejection rates may begin to stabilize or decline nationally in response to softening spot rates, giving carriers less incentive to reject contracted freight. This creates a tale of two markets: one of extreme capacity strain (reefer) and one of returning equilibrium (van/flatbed).
