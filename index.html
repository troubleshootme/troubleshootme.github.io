💰 Margin Strategy: How To Protect Spread In A Fuel Shock

  • Separate “rate” from “uncertainty.”

    • The linehaul is one issue.
    • The fuel volatility, detour exposure, and weekend coverage risk are separate issues.
    • Brokers lose money when they price all three like they are one static number.

  • Adopt a tiered quoting mindset today.

    • Tier 1 – Must-move / appointment-critical: Quote aggressively enough to secure first-call carrier commitment.
    • Tier 2 – Flexible but real: Quote with a narrow validity window and route caveats.
    • Tier 3 – Price shoppers: Give a realistic number and move on if the customer wants yesterday’s market.

  • Use short quote windows.

    • In this environment, 2-4 hour validity is reasonable for volatile lanes or longer-haul freight.
    • If a shipper wants you to honor a stale quote later, the answer is simple: the market moved, and your truck moved with it.

  • Push weekly fuel surcharge logic into shipper conversations now.

    • Large providers moving to weekly fuel surcharge updates is a signal that the market has stopped tolerating slower adjustment cycles.
    • Your customers do not need a lecture on energy markets. They need to understand that pricing cadence must speed up.

🚛 Carrier Desk Strategy: How To Win Trucks Without Overpaying Blindly

  • Prioritize carrier fit over board speed.

    • The cheapest carrier in a disrupted market often becomes the most expensive recovery.
    • Match trucks to freight based on lane comfort, fuel tolerance, and weather willingness.

  • Ask better questions on first contact.

    • Where did you last deliver?
    • What reload market do you want?
    • Will you run the flood detour or do you want to stay clear?
    • Are you willing to avoid I-80 if required?
    • Those answers tell you more than the first rate ask.

  • Use reload logic as leverage.

    • A carrier will sometimes take a slightly tighter linehaul if:
    • the destination is strong,
    • the next load is likely,
    • and your office has a reputation for clear dispatch.

  • Protect yourself operationally.

    • Confirm authority, insurance, driver identity, and any Electronic Logging Device (ELD) issues before dispatch.
    • In a volatile market, fraud and sloppy onboarding rise because everyone is moving faster.
