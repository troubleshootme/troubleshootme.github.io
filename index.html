🧭 Savvy Broker's Playbook

🔑 Executive Signal Summary

  • This is a real replacement-cost market, not a noisy one-day pop. Total available loads sit at 192,337, up 10.4% from 174,169, while the national average rate is $2.62/mile. More important than the headline is the structure underneath it: rates are higher than one week ago ($2.51/mile) and well above one month ago ($2.25/mile). That tells you this is not just emotional buying—it is capacity being repriced.

  • Diesel at $5.454/gallon is the market’s main discipline mechanism. When fuel is this high, carriers stop treating freight selection as a revenue decision and start treating it as a cash-flow survival decision. That changes behavior fast:

    • Owner-operators reject weak long-haul freight
    • Small fleets demand faster, cleaner turns
    • Deadhead tolerance collapses
    • Weather risk suddenly needs to be paid for, not merely explained

  • Open-deck is where the board is telling you to spend time. Flatbed (83,479) + Heavy Haul (40,332) + Specialized (22,234) = 146,045 loads, or 75.9% of the board. Even more telling, those same modes account for 68,824 of 80,366 moved loads, or 85.6% of actual movement. If your desk is still spending most of its energy on generic van coverage, you are likely under-allocating effort.

  • Reefer is the most dangerous mode to quote casually. Posted reefer is $2.78/mile, paid reefer is $3.04/mile. That +$0.26/mile spread is the clearest signal on the page that screen pricing is behind actual replacement cost. In other words: if you quote from the board before you secure capacity, you are volunteering margin.

  • Tomorrow morning matters more than tonight. The Indiana/Ohio storm zone and the Oregon/Nevada western disruption both create a classic next-cycle squeeze:

    • missed pickups
    • late arrivals
    • out-of-position trucks
    • dock compression in the first dispatch wave

    The highest-priced freight in the next 24 hours is likely to be short-haul recovery freight, repowers, and appointment-sensitive reloads.

🧠 What the market is really saying

  • The market is firming faster than customer budgets are updating. Compare the current tape to recent checkpoints:

    • Today: 192,337 loads at $2.62/mile
    • One week ago: 187,516 loads at $2.51/mile
    • One month ago: 167,676 loads at $2.25/mile

    That is a meaningful move in both demand and price floor. The deeper lesson: customers still thinking in February numbers are already behind the market.

  • Rising OTRI (Outbound Tender Rejection Index) confirms carrier confidence. Carriers do not reject contract freight aggressively unless they believe:

    • spot alternatives are real
    • replacement freight is paying better
    • capacity will stay tight long enough to matter

    That is what today’s environment looks like—especially in reefer, flatbed, and weather-exposed regional freight.

  • The market is rewarding turn efficiency, not just linehaul miles. With fuel at $5.454/gallon, a carrier’s decision process becomes:

    • How fast can I load?
    • How fast can I unload?
    • How much unpaid dwell risk am I taking?
    • Can I reload in a better market tomorrow?

    That is why short-haul premium freight, same-day recovery loads, and clean facility freight are outperforming generic mileage-based logic.

  • Weather is creating network drag, not just transit delay. The Midwest storm pattern and western mountain/wind pattern are affecting:

    • repositioning
    • reload timing
    • dock reliability
    • driver willingness
    • equipment utilization

    Smart brokers will price the next truck turn, not just the current move.

  • Lead with external facts, not internal frustration

    • Customers accept increases faster when you anchor to:
    • 192,337 total loads
    • $2.62/mile national average
    • $5.454/gallon diesel

    • Linehaul
    • Fuel
    • Timing/weather premium

    • it feels more rational

  • Push for pre-approvals before the truck hunt starts

    • detention
    • layover
    • repower exposure
