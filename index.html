📍 Primary Region Focus: Midwest

The Midwest is currently the most volatile and opportunity-rich region for freight brokers. Severe, widespread flooding along the Mississippi River is fracturing major transcontinental corridors (I-70, I-90), creating localized capacity vacuums. Simultaneously, the region is seeing a massive influx of flatbed and heavy haul demand for spring construction. This combination of high demand and restricted routing is driving significant detour premiums. Carriers are demanding hazard pay and higher rates to navigate the region, allowing savvy brokers to capture wider margins by accurately pricing the disruption.

🟧 Flatbed

  • Market read: Strongest mainstream opportunity today.
    • 88,058 loads
    • $3.23/mile paid
    • +$0.10 paid over posted
  • What it means:
    • This is no longer just “spring flatbed season.”
    • It is spring flatbed season plus Midwest routing friction plus fuel discipline.
  • Best moves today:
    • Separate standard flatbed from premium flatbed before quoting.

🤝 Carrier procurement strategy: how to secure trucks without buying dumb

  • Prioritize certainty over theoretical savings.

    • In a $5.65 diesel market, the wrong truck at a slightly lower rate is more expensive than the right truck at a fair rate.

  • What carriers care about most today:

    • Reload quality
    • Known route conditions
    • Clear detention terms
    • Fast pickup and unload realities
    • Whether you are hiding accessorial risk in linehaul

  • Best carrier-rep tactics today:

    • Sell the trip, not just the load.
    • Give routing logic
    • Identify likely crossing points
    • Show reload market strength after delivery
    • Use visible fuel and detour adders.
    • Carriers trust transparent math more than blended mystery numbers
    • Reconfirm assigned truck details close to pickup.
    • High-cost weeks increase the odds of swaps, fall-offs, and dispatch confusion

  • Best procurement sequencing today:

    1. Book must-cover Midwest service freight first
    2. Lock flatbed and heavy haul next
    3. Secure reefer before noon where appointments matter
    4. Use disciplined buying on routine van
    5. Backfill margin through partial matching where schedules allow

🛡️ Margin traps and how to avoid them

  • Trap 1: treating Midwest freight as a linehaul problem

    • Fix: quote in components:
    • Linehaul
    • Fuel
    • Detour / disruption
    • Detention / layover assumptions

  • Trap 2: chasing cheap open-deck trucks from the wrong geography

    • Fix: prioritize positioned carriers with reload logic, not just the lowest ask.

  • Trap 3: under-scoping heavy haul and specialized

    • Fix: verify dimensions, trailer type, permits, securement, loading method, and route constraints before committing.

  • Trap 4: overselling LTL / Partial transit times

    • Fix: sell cost relief with schedule flexibility, especially through weather-affected regions.

  • Trap 5: assuming van softness everywhere

    • Fix: distinguish between:
    • ordinary van freight
    • appointment-sensitive van freight
    • flood-affected van freight
