📊 Top-Line Summary

The national spot market is showing significant upward momentum to start the week, with total available loads climbing above 73,000 and a notable increase in paid rates across all major equipment types. The flatbed sector is experiencing a sharp rate correction upwards, reversing last week's softening trend and indicating renewed pricing power for carriers. Reefer capacity remains exceptionally tight with rates firming, driven by late-season agricultural demand. Van rates are also climbing, suggesting a broad-based increase in freight demand. Minor flood warnings persist in the Midwest and Southeast, but are not causing widespread disruptions to major freight corridors.

📈 National Data Dashboard

Vans The van market is demonstrating renewed strength, with average paid rates climbing to $2.00/mile, a notable increase from the end of last week. This rate appreciation is supported by a healthy volume of over 14,000 available loads, indicating that demand is currently outpacing readily available capacity. While the market isn't critically tight, the upward rate movement suggests that driver availability and equipment positioning are becoming more constrained. Brokers should anticipate continued rate pressure, especially on less desirable lanes, as carriers leverage the increased demand to be more selective.
Reefers The reefer market continues to be the tightest segment, with average paid rates firming at $2.41/mile. With just under 5,400 loads available nationally, demand for temperature-controlled equipment remains intense, fueled by late-season produce harvests in various regions and consistent demand from the grocery sector. The close alignment between posted and paid rates indicates that the market has fully priced in this capacity scarcity. Securing reliable reefer capacity requires aggressive booking strategies and a willingness to meet carrier rate expectations, as equipment is in high demand and carriers hold significant negotiating power.
Flatbeds The flatbed sector has seen a significant and rapid reversal of last week's pricing trends. Average paid rates have surged to $2.27/mile, erasing the previous dip and signaling a return to strong carrier leverage. This is driven by a massive volume of nearly 25,000 available loads, reflecting robust activity in the construction and industrial sectors. The 8-day trend of increasing load counts continues, confirming sustained demand. This environment creates significant opportunities for brokers, but also requires careful management of customer rate expectations as carrier pricing power is strong and capacity is being absorbed quickly.
Diesel Price $3.708/gal - The national average diesel price is holding steady at $3.708/gallon. This stability provides a predictable baseline for carrier costs, but the relatively high price continues to exert upward pressure on all-in rates and makes fuel surcharge accuracy critical in negotiations.
OTRI Trends Tender rejections are highest in the reefer and flatbed segments, where strong spot market rates provide carriers with lucrative alternatives to contract freight. The surge in flatbed demand and rates will likely drive rejections higher in that sector. Van rejection rates are expected to climb modestly in line with rising spot rates, particularly in regions with tightening capacity like the Southeast.

Major markets like Atlanta, Charlotte, and Jacksonville are experiencing high freight volumes due to strong manufacturing, construction, and port activity. This convergence of factors is creating significant rate volatility and providing brokers with prime opportunities for profitable loads, provided they can secure scarce capacity.

Atlanta, GA → Orlando, FL:

This high-volume lane is experiencing strong demand for both van and reefer equipment. Inbound freight to Florida is consistently higher than outbound, creating a capacity imbalance that drives rates up. Retail distribution into Central Florida and agricultural shipments are keeping trucks in high demand.

Birmingham, AL → Charlotte, NC:

This lane is at the heart of the booming flatbed market, connecting industrial and manufacturing hubs. Demand for flatbeds to move steel, building materials, and machinery is exceptionally high. The recent surge in national flatbed rates is acutely felt on this route.

Value Proposition: Our advantage is our ability to source reliable carriers in these tight conditions. Giving us additional lead time allows us to secure our most trusted partners for your freight, ensuring on-time service and minimizing your risk of a service failure.

Urgency Creator: With the pre-holiday retail inventory build-up also starting, van capacity is tightening as well. This will likely pull some drivers away from other sectors, making specialized equipment even harder to find as the week progresses.

Objection Handler: While the price reflects the current shortage, the greater risk is a service failure or not being able to move the freight at all. This rate ensures we can secure a committed, reliable carrier who won't fall off the load for a higher-paying alternative.
