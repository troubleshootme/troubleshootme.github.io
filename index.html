üìä Top-Line Summary

The national freight market is experiencing a surge in volume across all major equipment types, with total available loads climbing to over 115,000. Despite this increased demand, rate growth is uneven. Van and reefer paid rates are ticking up, indicating tightening capacity, while flatbed rates show signs of softening as the paid average falls below the posted average. Extreme heat continues to disrupt capacity in the Southwest and Southeast, creating significant challenges and premium rate opportunities for reefer freight. Meanwhile, severe thunderstorms and localized flooding in the Southeast and Midwest are causing short-term operational delays. Brokers should focus on the volatility in the reefer market for margin opportunities while capitalizing on high volumes in the more stable van and flatbed sectors.

Vans The van market is showing strong signs of life as available loads have increased to 19,878. This surge in demand is creating upward pressure on rates, with the average paid rate climbing to $1.84/mile, a full $0.10 above the average posted rate of $1.74/mile. While overall driver availability is improving nationally, regional disruptions from weather are creating spot shortages, forcing brokers to compete more aggressively on desirable lanes.
Reefers The reefer market remains highly volatile, driven by persistent, extreme heat across the southern states and a corresponding increase in available loads to 7,321. The average paid rate of $2.21/mile is slightly above the posted rate of $2.19/mile, reflecting a strained market where any available temperature-controlled unit is in high demand. The intense heat is not only boosting demand for climate-controlled goods but also stressing equipment and reducing driver willingness to operate in affected regions, leading to elevated tender rejections and premium rates on critical lanes.
Flatbeds Flatbed continues to dominate the market in sheer volume, with available loads reaching 37,149. However, a notable shift is occurring in rate dynamics; the average paid rate has settled at $2.20/mile, slightly below the average posted rate of $2.24/mile. This suggests that while demand from construction and industrial sectors is robust, capacity is currently sufficient to meet it, giving brokers a slight negotiating advantage. This presents an opportunity to secure capacity at or below posted rates, a contrast to the more inflationary van and reefer segments.
Diesel Price $3.74/gal - Diesel prices remain stable at $3.74/gallon. This predictability offers some relief to carriers facing other volatile costs, but the sustained high price continues to squeeze carrier margins, making accurate fuel surcharge calculations a critical component of load profitability.
OTRI Trends Tender rejections are likely rising for van and especially reefer equipment, as indicated by paid rates exceeding posted rates. Carriers are opting for more lucrative spot market opportunities, particularly in heat-affected regions where reefer demand is peaking. Flatbed rejection rates are expected to be more stable, as capacity is more aligned with current demand, giving carriers less incentive to refuse contracted loads.
