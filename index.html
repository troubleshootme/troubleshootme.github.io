📊 Top-Line Summary

The national spot freight market has officially broken out of its prolonged recessionary cycle today, driven by a massive 10.4% overnight surge in total available loads to 192,337. This volume explosion is colliding with a severe capacity constraint fueled by the national average diesel price holding at a punishing $5.454/gallon. Carriers are aggressively rejecting cheap freight, pushing the market average rate to $2.62/mile. With Outbound Tender Rejection Indices (OTRI) nearing 10%, brokers must pivot from a volume-first strategy to a margin-protection approach, securing capacity early in the dispatch cycle as severe weather across the Midwest and Pacific Northwest further fractures national routing networks.

Insight

Shorter, reload-friendly freight is clearing first

At $5.454 diesel, carrier selectivity is tilting away from headline rate-per mile and toward daily gross with reload certainty. Short-haul turns and lanes with dependable next loads are covering first, while long-haul freight with tight appointments or weak fuel treatment is far more exposed to late repricing and tender fallout.

  • Lead with incumbents and proven regionals. In a $5.454 diesel market, cheap unknown capacity frequently becomes expensive recovery freight.
