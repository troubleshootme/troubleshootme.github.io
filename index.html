📰 Impactful News Analysis

  1. FMCSA Mandates Electronic-Only Payments, Ending Paper Checks 🔗:
    Effective immediately, brokers must ensure all payments to the FMCSA for fines or fees are made electronically. This operational shift requires updating accounting procedures to avoid payment rejections and potential delays in operating authority reinstatement. Communicate this change to all accounting and compliance staff.
  2. Three ELD Models Decertified by FMCSA, Carriers Given 60 Days to Replace 🔗:
    Brokers must immediately verify that their carrier partners are not using the three recently revoked ELDs (ELDTRAK, GOLDEN ELD, and ALL-CAN). Carriers using these devices will be considered operating without an ELD after the grace period, posing a significant compliance risk. This is a critical talking point for carrier reps to confirm compliance during onboarding and dispatch.
  3. State Medical Marijuana Laws Complicating Driver Drug Testing and Recruitment 🔗:
    The conflict between federal DOT regulations (which prohibit marijuana use) and state-level legalization is shrinking the available driver pool, as seen in Oklahoma. This trend exacerbates the national driver shortage, tightening long-term capacity. Brokers should be aware that this is a contributing factor to rising driver costs and constrained labor supply.
