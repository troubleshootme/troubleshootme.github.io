🎯 Best Uses of Your Desk Time Today

1) Prioritize open-deck first

  • Why: That is where the board is largest and where seasonal construction/infrastructure demand is absorbing equipment fastest.
  • Broker play:
    • Work known carriers first, not cheapest board options.
    • Do not quote before confirming specs: tarp, securement, dimensions, weight, loading method, and site access.
    • Protect margin with precision. Bad spec intake on flatbed/heavy haul is more expensive than being slightly high on rate.

2) Pre-cover reefer before you sell it

  • Why: Reefer has the widest paid-versus-posted spread and is exposed to both produce season and western weather friction.
  • Broker play:
    • Secure truck first, then finalize customer number on Oregon/Washington or produce-adjacent freight.
    • Confirm reefer fuel responsibility, washout terms, and temperature instructions in writing.
    • Avoid hard promises on mountain-crossing transit unless you have a carrier already positioned and committed.

3) Use vans selectively, not passively

  • Why: Van spread is tight, so pure rate arbitrage is limited.
  • Broker play:
    • Chase short-haul, same-day, regional, and reload-friendly van freight.
    • Avoid long-haul van commitments that have thin FSC (fuel surcharge), tight appointments, or deadhead-heavy positioning.
    • Margin today in van comes from lane selection and service discipline, not heroic buy-side savings.

4) Use LTL/Partial as a retention tool

  • Why: Full truckload economics are harder to swallow for some customers when diesel is $5.454/gallon.
  • Broker play:
    • Offer LTL (Less Than Truckload) / partial where timing can stretch.
    • Use it to save the customer relationship, not as a blanket answer.
    • Be explicit that transit will be less predictable than dedicated truckload.
