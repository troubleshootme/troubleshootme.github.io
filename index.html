üìä Top-Line Summary

The national freight market is defined by a surge in demand, with load volumes showing a clear increasing trend across all major equipment types. However, this demand is met with significant operational headwinds as a widespread, severe heatwave blankets the southern and central United States. This is creating critical capacity shortages and rate volatility, particularly in the reefer market where paid rates are substantially outpacing posted averages. Brokers face a challenging environment where securing reliable capacity is the primary objective. While van and flatbed markets also see strong volume, the extreme heat is a universal risk factor, impacting driver availability, equipment reliability, and transit times nationwide.

üìà National Data Dashboard

Vans The van market is characterized by strong and increasing demand, with available loads currently at 16,126. The average paid rate of $1.85/mile is running slightly ahead of the posted average of $1.80/mile, indicating that while capacity is generally meeting demand, a premium is required to secure reliable carriers, especially in heat-affected regions. The rising load volumes suggest a healthy market, but brokers must factor in potential delays and higher carrier reluctance due to the extreme weather impacting driver availability and operational efficiency across the southern half of the country.
Reefers The reefer sector is under extreme pressure, directly driven by the severe heatwave. With available loads increasing and currently numbering 6,765, the market is intensely competitive. This is reflected in the significant spread between the average posted rate of $2.19/mile and the average paid rate, which has surged to $2.37/mile. This gap signals intense competition for scarce temperature-controlled equipment as heat stresses refrigeration units and reduces driver willingness to operate in dangerous conditions. Securing reefer capacity requires aggressive rate strategies and proactive communication with shippers about market realities.
Flatbeds The flatbed market continues to show robust activity, with load counts increasing and currently leading all equipment types at 28,231 available loads. The average paid rate of $2.28/mile is slightly above the posted average of $2.25/mile, suggesting a market that is strong but relatively stable compared to the volatile reefer sector. While demand from construction and industrial sectors remains high, capacity is largely keeping pace. However, the pervasive heat presents an operational risk, potentially impacting driver productivity and increasing the likelihood of equipment issues like tire blowouts.
Diesel Price $3.74/gal - The national average diesel price remains stable at $3.74/gallon. This stability offers a degree of predictability for carriers, but the sustained cost continues to exert pressure on operating margins, making accurate fuel surcharge calculations and negotiations a critical focus area for brokers.
OTRI Trends Tender rejection rates for reefer equipment are undoubtedly spiking across the South, Mid-South, and Southwest due to the dangerous heatwave, as carriers refuse loads in high-risk areas. The significant gap between paid and posted reefer rates confirms this trend. For van and flatbed, rejection rates are likely more stable but are elevated in the same heat-affected regions, as carriers may opt for shorter hauls or avoid areas with extreme temperatures. This creates a divided market where reefer capacity is highly unreliable, while van and flatbed capacity is more available but still subject to weather-related disruptions.
