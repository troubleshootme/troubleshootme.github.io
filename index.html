The freight market is exhibiting paradoxical behavior. This indicates the market is well-supplied for now, meeting demand from the construction and industrial sectors. However, the high volume of freight and the ongoing trend of carrier attrition suggest that any disruption, such as the current weather in the Midwest, could quickly tighten flatbed capacity and reverse this minor rate dip.
Diesel Price $3.741/gal - A marginal increase in diesel prices adds incremental pressure to carrier operating costs. OTRI Trends The Outbound Tender Rejection Index (OTRI) is rising, a critical indicator that capacity is tightening. Unusually, this is occurring while the Outbound Tender Volume Index (OTVI) is falling. This divergence signals that the capacity crunch is caused by carriers leaving the market (via fleet closures and authority revocations), not by an increase in freight demand. This gives remaining carriers more leverage to reject undesirable freight.
