📊 Top-Line Summary

The national freight market is defined by regional volatility today, driven by significant weather disruptions on the West Coast. While overall load volume has decreased from yesterday's peak, severe flooding in Southern California and a winter storm in the Sierra Nevada are creating acute capacity shortages and rate premiums in those regions. Reefer capacity remains exceptionally tight nationwide, with paid rates significantly outpacing posted rates, signaling urgent demand for temperature-controlled freight. In contrast, van and flatbed markets show signs of softening, with paid rates dipping below posted averages, offering negotiation opportunities for brokers in less weather-affected lanes. The primary focus for brokers should be capitalizing on the weather-induced chaos on the West Coast, where market imbalances present the greatest profit opportunities.

While the overall volume presents significant opportunity, the most profitable plays will be in specialized flatbed loads or lanes impacted by regional disruptions where capacity is less readily available.

Conversely, the negative paid-to-posted rate differential in the van and flatbed markets indicates that rejection rates in these sectors are likely stabilizing or declining, as carriers are more willing to accept posted rates to secure loads in a slightly softer market.
