üìä Top-Line Summary

The national freight market is experiencing a significant divergence in equipment trends this weekend. While overall load volumes show a healthy increase, a drop in total market value points to downward rate pressure, most acutely impacting the flatbed sector where paid rates have fallen sharply. Conversely, van and reefer rates are firming, with paid rates climbing due to regional pressures. Operationally, a severe heatwave across the Southeast is tightening temperature-controlled capacity and creating urgent demand, while persistent flooding in the Midwest continues to cause routing disruptions and displace equipment, presenting both risks and margin opportunities for brokers.

üìà National Data Dashboard

Vans The van sector is showing clear signs of tightening. Despite a slight dip in load counts from yesterday, the three-day trend shows increasing volume with 13,837 loads currently available. Critically, the average paid rate has jumped to $1.90/mile, now surpassing the average posted rate of $1.88/mile. This inversion indicates that available capacity is being secured at a premium, suggesting that regional disruptions and strong weekend demand are giving carriers greater pricing power. Brokers should expect firm rates and be prepared for competitive bidding on desirable lanes.
Reefers Reefer demand is intensifying, driven by seasonal produce and the severe heatwave in the Southeast. Available loads have increased, and the average paid rate has climbed to $2.21/mile. The spread between the posted rate ($2.28/mile) and the paid rate is narrowing, signaling that capacity is becoming more scarce and carriers are successfully negotiating higher rates. With extreme heat forecast to continue, the demand for temperature-controlled equipment will remain elevated, making it the most challenging but potentially highest-margin sector for brokers.
Flatbeds The flatbed market presents a major opportunity for brokers today. While load availability is the highest of all equipment types at 23,046 loads, the average paid rate has plummeted to $1.99/mile, a stark contrast to the average posted rate of $2.27/mile. This massive spread suggests a significant oversupply of capacity relative to current demand or a shift in freight mix, forcing carriers to accept lower-paying loads. This creates a prime environment for brokers to achieve significant margins by capitalizing on the discrepancy between shipper expectations and carrier realities.
Diesel Price $3.735/gal - The national average diesel price remains stable at a high level, continuing to exert pressure on carrier operating costs. This sustained cost environment makes carriers less willing to accept low rates, especially on long-haul trips, reinforcing the need for fairly priced loads with adequate fuel surcharges to secure reliable capacity.
OTRI Trends Tender rejection rates remain elevated, indicating ongoing carrier selectivity. This is not driven by overwhelming demand but by strategic positioning and cost avoidance. Carriers are actively rejecting unprofitable or inefficient loads, particularly in weather-affected regions. For brokers, this highlights the necessity of building strong carrier relationships and offering attractive, well-priced freight to ensure high acceptance rates.
