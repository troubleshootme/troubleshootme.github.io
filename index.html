  • Facility verification is mandatory in storm-affected freight

    • A truck can be on time and still become a loss if the facility is effectively shut down.
    • Action: reconfirm loading hours, delivery hours, yard conditions, and after-hours contacts before dispatch.

  • Compliance vetting needs to tighten, not loosen

    • With enforcement-data blind spots and financially stressed carriers, this is not the day to rely on a shallow vet.
    • Action: confirm:
    • MC (Motor Carrier) authority

    • ELD (Electronic Logging Device) status
    • driver identity
    • recent inspection signals where available
    • Reason: the wrong truck costs more than the expensive truck.

    • linehaul

    • weather / detour premium
    • Reason: it prevents later disputes and keeps customer expectations grounded in real replacement cost.

  • Do not sell standard transit on weather-exposed transcon freight

    • Action: use “estimated transit subject to weather and corridor restrictions” language where appropriate.
    • Reason: unrealistic service promises are the easiest way to turn gross margin into service failure.
