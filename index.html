Capacity is tight, particularly out of agricultural regions, and equipment is positioned to service the most profitable lanes, leading to higher-than-average tender rejections for less desirable freight. Brokers must be prepared to pay a premium and provide quick turnarounds to secure reliable reefer capacity in this environment.
Flatbeds Despite a robust volume of over 30,000 available loads, the flatbed market is advantageous for brokers. Real-time paid rates ($2.25/mile) are trending below posted rates ($2.28/mile), indicating that capacity is outpacing even the strong demand from the construction and industrial sectors. This creates a competitive environment where carriers are willing to negotiate down from initial asking prices to secure freight. This contrasts with week-old data that showed higher national averages, confirming a continued softening trend and providing significant negotiation leverage for brokers.
Diesel Price $3.729/gal - The national diesel average remains stable, holding just below $3.73/gallon. This stability provides some predictability for fuel surcharges, but the elevated price level continues to be a major component of carrier operating costs, limiting their flexibility on all-in rates.
OTRI Trends Tender rejection trends are diverging sharply. Reefer rejections remain elevated as carriers chase premium spot rates in high-demand agricultural regions. Van rejections are low but may begin to inch up on certain lanes as carriers gain minor pricing power. Flatbed rejections are very low, as ample capacity and competitive pressure incentivize carriers to accept tendered loads.
