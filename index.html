Translation: the board got bigger, but clearing got less efficient. That creates selective buying opportunities on generic freight while true scarcity remains concentrated in reefer and open-deck.

Those same segments account for 46,465 moved loads, or about 80.7% of moved volume. If a desk is still spending most of its time on dry van, it is misallocated relative to today’s revenue pool.

  • Reefer remains the cleanest scarcity signal on the board. Reefer paid $2.82 vs. posted $2.60 = +$0.22/mile. That is still the widest positive paid-versus-posted spread in the market. In practical terms: if the load is temp-sensitive, heat-exposed, or appointment-sensitive, buy first and quote second.

  • Van is balanced, not broken, and not a premium segment by default. Van paid $2.25 vs. posted $2.24 = +$0.01/mile on 24,149 loads. That is a very different signal from reefer. Do not import reefer psychology into van pricing. Negotiate on dense, reload-friendly freight and reserve premium buying for weather-exposed or operationally ugly van loads.

  • Specialized is today’s best negotiation pocket. Specialized paid $2.49 vs. posted $2.58 = -$0.09/mile. That is the market telling you some posted asks are aspirational rather than executable. If the dimensions, loading method, and route are clear, there is room to press buy-side cost.

  • Diesel at $5.099/gallon is still the market’s hidden tax. More loads on the board do not mean cheaper coverage if the lane has poor reloads, long deadhead, heavy idling, mountains, or weather friction. Separate linehaul from FSC (Fuel Surcharge) and shorten quote validity windows.
