🗺️ Regional & Lane Analysis

📍 Primary Region Focus: Southeast US

The Southeast freight market is currently a hotbed of volatility and opportunity for brokers. Driven by surging port volumes, early agricultural staging, and a rapid spike in regional diesel prices (now nearing $5/gallon in states like Alabama), capacity is structurally tightening. Carriers are becoming highly selective, preferring short-haul, high-yield port drayage or regional runs over long-haul transcontinental freight that exposes them to massive fuel risk. This dynamic is pushing outbound rates higher, particularly on lanes exiting major freight hubs like Atlanta, Savannah, and Jacksonville.

🛣️ Key Lane Watch

Atlanta, GA → Miami, FL:

This quintessential consumption lane is experiencing significant rate pressure as carriers demand heavy premiums to enter the South Florida market. With diesel over $5.159/gallon, the cost of deadheading out of Miami is too high for carriers to absorb, forcing the inbound rate to cover the round-trip fuel expense. Produce season is beginning to offer some outbound relief, but reefer capacity is currently prioritizing higher-yield local runs.

Mobile, AL → Charlotte, NC:

Driven by the surge in Alabama diesel prices and robust industrial/port demand, this lane is seeing intense competition for flatbed and heavy-haul equipment. Carriers are pushing back on heavy loads that reduce fuel efficiency, causing a backlog of industrial freight. The impending inland fuel surcharges from ocean carriers are also pushing more freight into the spot market for transloading.

Regional Insight

South Florida remains a round-trip market, not a one-way market

Atlanta-to-Miami pricing is still being set by outbound uncertainty in Florida, not by the inbound headhaul alone. Until produce and local reload options become consistent enough to offset deadhead and fuel burn, carriers will continue to underwrite the lane as a round-trip commitment, which keeps Friday-through-Monday tenders especially exposed to repricing and fallout.
