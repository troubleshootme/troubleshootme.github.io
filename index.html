The Midwest is currently the most volatile freight region in the country, caught between massive industrial flatbed demand and severe weather disruptions. Flash flooding in Ohio and Indiana is forcing widespread local detours, while tornado watches in Missouri and Kansas are threatening major East-West transcontinental corridors. Simultaneously, the region is seeing heavy outbound volume as manufacturing facilities push spring inventory. Carriers are utilizing this combination of high demand and hazardous operating conditions to push rates significantly above historical averages.

💵 Pricing Rules for Today

  • Rule 1: Quote for trip economics, not lane history

    • Past averages matter less when diesel is $5.49/gal and weather is removing hours from the day.
    • Carriers are pricing:
    • loaded miles
    • deadhead
    • fuel burn
    • idle time
    • route detours
    • reload probability

  • Rule 2: Shorten quote validity on volatile lanes

    • Same-day validity is appropriate on:
    • Southern Plains freight
    • Ohio Valley flood-affected freight
    • Pacific Northwest freight tied to regional fuel pain
    • Reefer and open-deck appointment-sensitive loads

  • Rule 3: Separate fuel and execution risk in your customer conversation

    • Shippers resist “rate increases”
    • They understand:
    • fuel-driven operating cost
    • weather-driven service risk
    • facility-driven dwell cost
    • The best brokers unbundle the why

  • Rule 4: Paid-posted spreads tell you where negotiation room actually exists

    • Reefer +$0.27: room exists only if you priced the risk correctly
    • Van +$0.05: little room; avoid false margin assumptions
    • Flatbed +$0.06: volume is strong, but not sloppy
    • Heavy haul -$0.02: board optimism is not executable pricing
    • LTL/Partial -$0.06: good cost-control tool, weaker service precision

  • Rule 5: Protect gross margin dollars, not ego

    • In a market like this, the best brokers often win by:
    • holding fewer bad loads
    • avoiding service failures
    • defending clean premium freight
    • A smaller clean book beats a bigger rescue book bought wrong

🤝 Carrier Desk Tactics That Will Win Today

  • Lead with trip quality

    • Carriers under fuel pressure respond better to:
    • fast loading
    • clear detention terms
    • reload visibility
    • accurate appointment windows
    • weather-aware routing
    • They respond worse to:
    • “That’s the market”
    • vague pickup times
    • hidden tarping or detention fights

  • Use your best carriers on the hardest freight, not the cheapest freight

    • Save proven carriers for:
    • reefer appointments
    • open-deck project freight
    • Ohio Valley delivery-sensitive loads
    • Southern Plains storm-exposed lanes

  • Add compliance to your dispatch quality filter

    • With ELD (Electronic Logging Device) revocations in play, the cheapest truck may be a non-starter.
    • Operational rule:
    • verify carrier
    • verify assigned driver identity close to pickup
    • verify ELD compliance status
    • verify route plan for weather-exposed freight

  • Book earlier than usual on weather lanes

    • The carrier psychology today is simple:
    • good freight gets accepted early
    • risky freight gets repriced later
    • If you wait for an afternoon “deal,” you are usually waiting for a worse service outcome.
