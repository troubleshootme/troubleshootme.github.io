üìä Top-Line Summary

The market begins the week with a clear divergence across equipment types. Van capacity is tightening, with real-time paid rates now exceeding posted rates, signaling a shift in pricing power back to carriers. In contrast, the flatbed market remains deeply oversupplied, presenting significant cost-saving opportunities for shippers and margin potential for brokers who can leverage the rate softness. The reefer market is stabilizing after recent volatility, with rates settling slightly below weekend highs. The Southeast remains the focal point for opportunity, offering a dynamic mix of firm reefer demand and high-volume, competitively priced flatbed freight.

üìà National Data Dashboard

Vans The van market is showing clear signs of firming to start the week. Real-time paid rates are averaging $1.90/mile, now outpacing the average posted rate of $1.86/mile. This inversion indicates that available capacity is being absorbed quickly, forcing brokers to bid up to secure trucks and eroding carrier negotiation flexibility. While current rates are still below the week-old benchmark of $1.96/mile, the immediate momentum is upward. With over 17,000 loads visible on real-time platforms, demand is healthy, and brokers must act with urgency on key lanes before rates climb further through the day.
Reefers The reefer market is finding a new equilibrium, with capacity and demand appearing more balanced than in previous days. Current paid rates of $2.27/mile are tracking just slightly below the average posted rate of $2.28/mile, suggesting a minor cooling and a shift to a more neutral negotiating environment. This is a slight step down from the week-old average of $2.31/mile. While strong seasonal produce movements in the Southeast and West Coast continue to provide a floor for rates, the initial urgency has subsided, giving brokers a narrow window to secure capacity without paying a significant premium.
Flatbeds The flatbed sector remains an exceptionally soft, broker-favorable market characterized by immense overcapacity. With over 36,000 loads available in real-time, equipment is abundant, driving intense competition among carriers. Paid rates are averaging $2.25/mile, notably below the average posted rate of $2.27/mile and drastically lower than the week-old benchmark of $2.55/mile. This confirms a sustained downturn in flatbed pricing, providing brokers with substantial leverage to negotiate favorable rates and secure high margins, particularly on lanes where demand from construction or industrial sectors has not kept pace with truck supply.
Diesel Price $3.722/gal - The national average for diesel has decreased slightly to $3.722/gallon. This minor drop offers marginal cost relief to carriers but is not substantial enough to fundamentally alter rate negotiations. It does, however, remove 'rising fuel costs' as an immediate justification for rate hikes from carriers.
OTRI Trends Tender rejection trends are diverging. Van rejection rates are likely to inch upward as the firming spot market makes contract freight less appealing for carriers. Reefer rejections should remain stable as the market finds its balance. Flatbed rejection rates will continue to scrape bottom, as the vast oversupply of capacity incentivizes carriers to accept nearly all tendered loads to maintain revenue and utilization.
