üìà National Data Dashboard

Vans The van market is showing clear signs of tightening as the week begins. Real-time paid rates are averaging $1.90/mile, now surpassing the average posted rate of $1.86/mile. This inversion indicates that available capacity, currently reflected in over 22,000 available loads, is being absorbed quickly, forcing brokers to pay above initial asking prices to secure trucks. This firming trend contrasts with week-old data that showed a higher national average, suggesting the market is rebounding from a recent dip. Driver availability is becoming constrained in key freight hubs, particularly in the Southeast, putting upward pressure on rates for desirable lanes.
Reefers The reefer market is hot, with strong demand driven by peak agricultural seasons in the Southeast and West Coast. Paid rates are averaging $2.29/mile, significantly outpacing the average posted rate of $2.21/mile, which points to a competitive market with tight capacity. With just over 10,000 loads available, the demand for temperature-controlled equipment is outstripping supply, giving carriers substantial pricing leverage. This is a sharp contrast to the softer conditions seen intermittently last week, and brokers must be prepared to pay a premium for reliable reefer capacity, especially on lanes originating from produce-rich regions.
Flatbeds The flatbed sector remains a broker's market, characterized by ample capacity and downward pressure on rates. Despite a high volume of over 38,000 available loads, paid rates are averaging $2.25/mile, slightly below the average posted rate of $2.27/mile. This suggests carriers are willing to negotiate down to secure freight amidst soft demand from the construction and industrial sectors. This environment provides significant margin opportunities for brokers who can leverage the widespread equipment availability. While week-old data showed a much higher national average of $2.55/mile, the current real-time data confirms a sustained cooling trend.
Diesel Price $3.722/gal - The national average for diesel has slightly decreased to $3.722/gallon. However, this is a temporary reprieve, as market reports forecast a significant price increase effective Tuesday. Brokers should advise carriers to fuel today and factor higher fuel surcharges into quotes for the remainder of the week.
OTRI Trends Tender rejections for van and reefer loads are expected to climb this week as the spot market firms, making spot freight more lucrative for carriers than contract loads. Rejection rates will be highest in the Southeast. Conversely, flatbed tender rejections will remain exceptionally low as carriers in the oversupplied market are incentivized to accept all tendered freight to maintain utilization.
