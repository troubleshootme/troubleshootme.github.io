üìä Top-Line Summary

The national freight market is experiencing a notable increase in load volumes across all major equipment types, signaling a busy end to July. Despite this, the market shows signs of rate pressure, with the average paid rate of $1.91/mile only slightly above the posted average of $1.85/mile. This narrow gap suggests that while capacity is tightening enough to command a small premium, the overall market has not yet reached a point of widespread rate inflation. Reefers The reefer market is defined by weather-driven demand and capacity stress. With 11,638 loads available and volumes increasing, the severe heatwave across the southern and central U.S. is the primary market driver. This has pushed the average paid rate to $2.29/mile, exceeding the posted average of $2.26/mile, as shippers urgently seek temperature-controlled capacity to protect sensitive goods. The extreme heat impacts equipment performance and reduces driver productivity, effectively shrinking the available pool of reliable capacity and keeping rates firm, especially on lanes originating from or moving through the affected regions.
Flatbeds The flatbed sector remains the most active by volume, with 44,825 available loads and a strong upward trend in freight availability. The market appears balanced and stable, with the average paid rate of $2.26/mile nearly identical to the posted average of $2.27/mile. This indicates that capacity is largely keeping pace with robust demand from the construction and industrial sectors. For brokers, the opportunity lies in the high volume of available freight, allowing for consistent booking opportunities without the extreme rate volatility currently seen in the reefer sector.
Diesel Price $3.734/gal - The national average diesel price has decreased slightly to $3.734/gallon. While any reduction offers minor relief to carrier operating costs, the price remains elevated, continuing to influence carrier profitability and rate negotiations. This small dip is unlikely to cause a significant change in carrier behavior or fuel surcharge calculations.
OTRI Trends Tender rejections are highest in the Southeast and Midwest, directly correlated with the extreme heatwave. Carriers are increasingly rejecting loads in these regions due to operational risks to drivers and equipment, as well as more attractive rates on less strenuous lanes. Flooding in parts of the Midwest and Mountain West is also contributing to rejections, creating localized capacity challenges and necessitating higher rates to secure commitments.
