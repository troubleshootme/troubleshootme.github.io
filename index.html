🧭 Savvy Broker's Playbook

🔑 Executive Signal Summary

  • This is a capacity-tension market, not a “big board = cheap trucks” market. 191,302 total loads with a $2.45/mile national average tells you freight is plentiful, but the real story is that carriers are becoming more selective because diesel is $5.099/gallon.

  • Execution is tightening even though volume expanded. Total loads rose +1.3% to 191,302, but loads moved fell to 57,571 from 62,292. That’s a classic sign of more posted freight, slower clearing, and higher rate sensitivity.

  • Open-deck is still the center of gravity for broker time. Flatbed + Heavy Haul + Specialized = 147,236 loads, or about 76.9% of the board. Those same segments account for 46,465 moved loads, or about 80.8% of moved volume. If your desk is not tilted toward open-deck, you are under-allocating to the dominant revenue pool.

  • Reefer is the sharpest scarcity play. Reefer paid at $2.82/mile vs. posted at $2.60/mile gives a +$0.22/mile spread. Add Southwest extreme heat and the market is telling you: buy early, or pay up later.

  • Van is not loose; it is selective. Van paid at $2.25/mile vs. posted at $2.24/mile is nearly flat, but that does not mean easy coverage. It means the lane has to be clean, reload-friendly, and fuel-aware.

  • Specialized is the best visible negotiation pocket. Specialized paid at $2.49/mile vs. posted at $2.58/mile creates a -$0.09/mile spread. That is where disciplined brokers can still press rate—if specs are complete and routing is simple.

📊 What the board is really saying

  • The market is wider, but not easier. More loads are available, but carriers are asking: “What happens after this load?” That means deadhead, reload potential, fuel burn, and weather exposure matter more than raw board count.

  • Posted rates are lagging true replacement cost. The visible board is not yet fully reflecting diesel at $5.099/gallon, especially on lanes that trap trucks in Florida, push them through heat-stressed Southwest freight, or create weather-delay risk.

  • Shippers will anchor to the board; carriers will anchor to fuel. Your edge is translating the gap between those two mindsets. Shippers see volume. Carriers see survival math.

  • Clearing quality matters more than headline abundance. With 57,571 moved loads against 191,302 available, the market is functioning like a selective auction. The best freight clears; the weakest freight sits.

💰 Fuel shock: how to sell and buy in a $5.099 diesel market

  • **Diesel is now a capacity tax. At $5.099/gallon, carriers are recalculating every move around fuel burn, deadhead, and reload probability.

  • **Separate fuel from linehaul wherever possible. Transparent fuel surcharge (FSC) language is cleaner than pretending the base rate alone can absorb the shock.

  • **Shorten quote validity windows. On long-haul, reload-sensitive, or weather-exposed freight, today’s rate can be stale fast.

  • **Stop buying “cheap” empty miles. If a lane traps a truck in Florida or forces long repositioning after a drop, the carrier will price that pain immediately.

  • **Use fuel as a negotiation reset. The cleanest conversations right now are: “Here’s the freight, here’s the fuel reality, here’s the reload plan.”
