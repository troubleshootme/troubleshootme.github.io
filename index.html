Reefers The reefer segment is the tightest sector of the market, with strong upward pressure on rates. Real-time paid rates have climbed to $2.27/mile, significantly outpacing the average posted rate of $2.23/mile. This wide spread, across more than 11,000 available loads, signals intense competition for limited equipment and gives carriers substantial pricing leverage. This tightness is fueled by peak produce seasons in key agricultural regions. Current rates are approaching the higher week-old national average of $2.31/mile, indicating the market is returning to peak pricing levels and brokers must be prepared to pay a premium for reliable capacity.
Flatbeds The flatbed market remains saturated with capacity, creating significant opportunities for brokers. Despite a high volume of over 45,000 available loads, paid rates are averaging $2.25/mile, slightly below the average posted rate of $2.26/mile. This confirms a soft, broker-favorable environment where carriers are competing for freight. Current real-time rates are substantially lower than the week-old national average of $2.55/mile, highlighting a sustained downward trend. This provides brokers with strong negotiation leverage and the ability to offer competitive pricing to customers in the industrial and construction sectors.
Diesel Price $3.722/gal - The national average for diesel has slightly decreased to $3.722/gallon. However, industry reports project a notable price increase beginning tomorrow, which will raise carrier operating costs. Brokers should proactively address this by securing all-in rates today or discussing flexible fuel surcharges with customers.
OTRI Trends Tender rejections are diverging. Reefer rejection rates will remain elevated and may climb further as carriers opt for lucrative spot market opportunities. Van rejections are likely to hold steady as contract and spot rates find a temporary equilibrium. Flatbed rejections will continue to be exceptionally low, as ample capacity and soft rates mean carriers are accepting nearly all tendered freight to maintain utilization.
