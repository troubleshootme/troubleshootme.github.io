Vans The van market continues to tighten, with real-time paid rates at $1.90/mile now edging above average posted rates of $1.89/mile. Brokers must act decisively, as hesitation in booking will likely result in higher costs and difficulty securing equipment.
Reefers The reefer market is characterized by expensive stability, with both paid and posted rates holding firm at $2.29/mile. This rate is slightly below the week-old national average of $2.31/mile, suggesting the market has reached a high plateau rather than continuing to climb. With over 6,800 loads available, demand from ongoing produce seasons, particularly in the Southeast and West Coast, keeps equipment utilized and limits deadhead, supporting premium rates. Brokers with strong carrier relationships in key agricultural regions will have a distinct advantage in sourcing reliable capacity.
Flatbeds The flatbed segment is exceptionally robust, with paid rates ($2.27/mile) now surpassing posted rates ($2.25/mile), indicating immediate upward rate pressure. This is occurring in a high-volume environment, with over 35,800 loads available, driven by strong industrial and construction demand. Although current rates are tracking below the week-old national average of $2.37/mile, the real-time data points to a market that is heating up rapidly. The sheer volume of freight creates significant opportunity, but the rising paid rates mean brokers must be aggressive in quoting to protect margins.

This slight increase adds pressure to carrier operating costs, reinforcing their need to secure higher line-haul rates to maintain profitability. This factor further reduces carrier flexibility in rate negotiations.
OTRI Trends Tender rejection rates are likely to increase for van freight as the spot market firms and paid rates become more attractive than contract rates. Flatbed rejections may also see a slight uptick in high-demand regions as carriers capitalize on rising spot opportunities. Reefer rejection rates are expected to remain stable but elevated, reflecting a market where capacity is well-aligned with high-paying seasonal demand.
