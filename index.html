News Insight

FMCSA Fraud: Three Checks That Actually Catch Purchased Authorities

The USDOT number transfer crackdown is urgent but needs to translate into specific desk actions. The fraud pattern typically involves a recently reactivated or transferred authority paired with mismatched insurance and address records.

  • In SAFER, check the MC authority's original registration date — flag any carrier with an operating authority less than 90 days old or showing a recent reinstatement.
  • Confirm the physical operating address matches the carrier's dispatch contact and insurance domicile, not a virtual office or mail-forwarding address.
  • Pull insurance certificates directly from FMCSA's insurance record rather than accepting a carrier-supplied COI — purchased authorities frequently carry lapsed or mismatched coverage.
