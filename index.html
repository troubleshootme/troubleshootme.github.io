⛽ Fuel is no longer an accessorial detail — it is the market structure

  • Diesel at $5.099/gallon is creating a hard behavioral floor.

    • Carriers are making different decisions now:
    • rejecting lower-yield freight
    • demanding fuel to be broken out explicitly
    • choosing shorter, cleaner, reload-friendly trips
    • refusing ambiguous detention and layover exposure

  • What this means for broker behavior:

    • All-in quoting is becoming dangerous.
    • A single flat price ages too fast in this market.
    • Linehaul and fuel should be separated on new spot quotes whenever the customer relationship allows it.

  • The customer psychology piece:

    • Many shippers still believe a fuel event should be absorbed by “the market.”
    • In reality, the market already absorbed it by becoming more selective.
    • The winning message is not:
    • “Rates are up.”
    • It is:
    • “Coverage certainty now depends on pricing that reflects today’s fuel, not yesterday’s assumptions.”

  • The carrier psychology piece:

    • In a $5.099 diesel market, a carrier is not just asking:
    • “What does this load pay?”
    • They are asking:
    • “What does this trip net after fuel, deadhead, delay risk, and reload uncertainty?”
    • That is why reload storytelling matters so much today, especially out of Arizona and other expensive-position markets.
