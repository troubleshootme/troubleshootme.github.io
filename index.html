üìä Top-Line Summary

The national freight market is experiencing significant tightening, particularly in the van and reefer sectors, where paid rates are outpacing posted averages, signaling strong demand against constrained capacity. A dangerous and widespread heatwave across the Southeast and Midwest is the primary market driver, severely stressing reefer capacity and creating operational hazards. Flatbed remains robust with high volume, though rates show more balance. Economic headwinds persist with ongoing tariff discussions creating uncertainty, while major carriers' earnings reports reflect a challenging but resilient operating environment.

üìà National Data Dashboard

Vans The van market is showing clear signs of tightening as demand outpaces readily available capacity. With available loads increasing and currently at 24,366, the most telling metric is the rate inversion: the average paid rate of $1.92/mile is now $0.07 higher than the average posted rate of $1.85/mile. This spread indicates that brokers are paying premiums to secure trucks, reflecting a market where carriers have increasing leverage. This condition is exacerbated by extreme heat in the Southeast, which is beginning to impact driver availability and equipment positioning, pushing capacity tighter on key lanes.
Reefers Reefer capacity is under significant strain, with paid rates ($2.32/mile) climbing well above posted rates ($2.23/mile), creating the largest premium of any equipment type. This is a direct consequence of the severe heatwave blanketing major agricultural and freight hubs in the Southeast and Midwest, which boosts demand for temperature-controlled services while simultaneously creating difficult operating conditions for drivers and equipment. With an increasing trend in available loads, currently at 10,765, the market is highly volatile, and brokers must be prepared for continued rate pressure and capacity shortages on temperature-sensitive freight.
Flatbeds The flatbed sector continues to be the market leader in volume, with 39,062 available loads. However, unlike other equipment types, the rate environment appears more balanced and competitive. The average paid rate of $2.26/mile is slightly below the average posted rate of $2.29/mile, suggesting that while demand from construction and industrial sectors is strong and increasing, capacity is more readily available. This creates an opportunity for brokers to find value, but they must contend with high overall volume and competition for quality carriers.
Diesel Price $3.734/gal - The national average diesel price has ticked up slightly to $3.734/gallon. This high, stable cost base continues to pressure carrier margins, making them more selective on loads and firm on rate negotiations, particularly on longer-haul routes.
OTRI Trends Tender rejections are rising, especially in the Southeast and Midwest. The combination of extreme heat, which poses a safety risk and strains equipment, and scattered severe thunderstorms is causing carriers to reject more loads. This trend is validated by the widening gap between paid and posted rates for van and reefer, indicating that securing capacity requires higher premiums.
