🧭 Savvy Broker's Playbook

🔑 Executive Signal Summary

  • This is a tighter execution market than the headline volume drop suggests.

    • Total available loads fell to 188,298, down 3.1% from 194,353, but the national average rate held at $2.71/mile.
    • More importantly, loads moved rose to 70,363 from 68,490. That means the board looks slightly softer, but the market is actually clearing freight more aggressively.

  • The center of gravity is still industrial and open-deck.

    • Flatbed, heavy haul, and specialized combine for 151,062 loads, which is about 80.2% of visible freight.
    • Those same modes account for 61,389 of 70,363 moved loads, or about 87.2% of executed volume.
    • Translation: today’s best margin is still in construction, machinery, project, and industrial freight.

  • Diesel at $5.614/gal is the real pricing floor.

    • Carriers are not merely asking for more money.
    • They are refusing bad trip design: unpaid detours, slow facilities, uncertain reloads, and vague appointment windows.

  • Weather is extending premiums, not flushing them out.

    • Midwest flooding and Friday rain risk mean routing friction will likely hold into Monday.
    • Wyoming wind will likely show up as downstream eastbound tightness out of Utah and Colorado, not just as a local disruption.

  • This is a selective premium market, not a universal blowout.

    • Flatbed, heavy haul, van, and reefer are all settling above posted rates.
    • Specialized and LTL (Less Than Truckload) / partial are the two clearest negotiation pockets if the freight is clean and well-scoped.

📈 What the market is really saying

  • The market is stable in volume, but firmer in cost structure.

    • One week ago: 190,781 loads at $2.69/mile
    • One month ago: 189,310 loads at $2.44/mile
    • Today: 188,298 loads at $2.71/mile
    • That is the key pattern: roughly similar load count, materially higher rate environment. The real change is not demand exploding. It is active capacity becoming more selective and more expensive to deploy.

  • Fewer loads on the board does not mean easier buying.

    • Van volume fell to 20,374, but paid is $2.52/mile vs. $2.45/mile posted.
    • Reefer volume fell to 7,114, but paid is $2.80/mile vs. $2.75/mile posted.
    • When volume drops and paid rates still beat posted rates, that usually means carriers are rejecting weak freight and gravitating toward better trip economics.

  • Flatbed remains the most scalable mainstream margin play.

    • 89,216 flatbed loads
    • $3.13/mile posted
    • $3.22/mile paid
    • The spread is telling you that screen prices are still underestimating actual buying cost on quality open-deck freight.

  • Heavy haul is still premium, but precision matters more than enthusiasm.

    • 41,888 loads
    • $3.19/mile posted
    • $3.26/mile paid
    • This is not a lane-shopping market. It is an execution-detail market.

  • Specialized is tighter than it looks, but not every load deserves a premium buy.

    • 19,958 loads
    • $2.87/mile posted
    • $2.83/mile paid
    • That negative spread tells me some brokers or shippers are over-posting to attract niche equipment, but truly clean, clearly defined loads are settling lower.
    • The opportunity is scope discipline, not blind rate aggression.

  • LTL/partial is a strategic account-defense tool today.

    • 9,748 loads
    • $1.76/mile posted
    • $1.72/mile paid
    • That is one of the few places where a broker can save a margin-sensitive customer money without taking service risk that is out of proportion to the reward.

🚛 Mode-by-mode broker playbook

🟧 Flatbed

  • Market read: Still the best broad-based revenue pocket on the board

    • Strong demand plus flood detours plus diesel discipline keeps this market elevated.

  • What to do today

    • Split flatbed quotes into two buckets
    • Standard freight: legal, easy access, no tarp, no jobsite complexity
    • Premium freight: tarp, jobsite, limited parking, detour exposure, crane coordination
    • Pre-buy positioned trucks for Midwest and lower Midwest freight before Friday weather creates another round of turn-time drag.
    • Quote detours and dwell separately, not inside one all-in number.

  • What to avoid

    • Do not treat all open-deck freight as interchangeable.
    • A clean steel load and a muddy construction-site delivery are not the same buy.

🏗️ Heavy Haul

  • Market read: High-paying, but margin can disappear fast if routing is under-scoped

    • Flooded corridors matter more here because alternate permit paths can get expensive quickly.

  • What to do today

    • Confirm before quoting
    • trailer type
    • exact dimensions
    • axle needs
    • permit path
    • escort requirements
    • loading and unloading method
    • alternate route feasibility
    • Use reroute language in writing before tender.

  • What to avoid

    • Never quote from a shorthand commodity description alone.
    • “Equipment” is not a quoteable spec.

🧊 Reefer

  • Market read: Quietly tight and likely tighter by afternoon

    • 7,114 loads is not a huge board count, and $2.80 paid tells you carriers still have leverage.
    • Western freeze warnings are keeping protect-from-freeze (PFF, Protect From Freeze) freight expensive.

  • What to do today

    • Cover reefer earlier than usual, especially for:
    • produce-related loads
    • hard appointment freight
    • western PFF freight
    • any load with temperature sensitivity but sloppy shipper instructions
    • Ask exact temperature and protection requirements before posting.

  • What to avoid

    • Do not post vague “reefer needed” freight and try to sort out temperature details later.
    • That invites repricing and service failures.

🚐 Dry Van

  • Market read: Firm, selective, and stronger than the board count alone suggests

    • 20,374 loads
    • $2.52 paid vs. $2.45 posted
    • Van is not the hottest mode, but it is absolutely not loose where service matters.

  • What to do today

    • Pay up only where failure is expensive
    • flood-affected Midwest
    • retail replenishment
    • automotive parts
    • high-penalty appointments
    • same-day or next-morning pickups
    • Stay disciplined on ordinary freight, especially where the Southeast offers cleaner operating conditions.

  • What to avoid

    • Do not let the flatbed market distort your van buying.
    • Routine van freight still requires discipline.

🟪 Specialized

  • Market read: A scoping market more than a rate market

    • Paid is below posted, which means sloppy public-board pricing is out ahead of real settlement.

  • What to do today

    • Validate exactly what “specialized” means
    • trailer type
    • securement
    • insurance level
    • site access
    • loading method
    • dimensional profile
    • Negotiate if the load is truly clean and repeatable.

  • What to avoid

    • Do not overbuy based on the commodity name alone.
    • In specialized, the difference between “needs a niche trailer” and “posted as niche by habit” is where margin lives.

📦 LTL/Partial

  • Market read: Best cost-defense tool for price-sensitive accounts

    • $1.72 paid vs. $1.76 posted gives brokers actual buying leverage.

  • What to do today

    • Push consolidation for shippers resisting full truckload rates.
    • Build regional co-loads where density allows.
    • Sell flexibility honestly on ETA through weather-affected corridors.

  • What to avoid

    • Do not oversell precision transit through Midwest disruptions.
    • The savings are real; false certainty is expensive.

💵 Pricing strategy that protects margin today

  • Use component pricing on disrupted freight

    • Linehaul
    • Fuel surcharge tied to $5.614 diesel
    • Out-of-route or detour miles
    • Weather-related dwell
    • Tarp / securement / permit / escort / PFF as applicable

  • Why this works

    • Shippers push back hardest when they feel the price is arbitrary.
    • They push back less when the price is structured and evidence-based.

  • What to say to shippers

    • “The board is showing fewer loads, but actual execution is tighter. We’re seeing stronger paid rates, slower turns, and more carrier selectivity. We can protect service best by pricing the risk elements transparently.”

  • What to say to carriers

    • Routine van can carry slightly longer validity
    • Premium reefer and open-deck should be rechecked close to pickup
