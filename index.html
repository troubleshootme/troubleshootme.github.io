Flatbeds The flatbed sector is exceptionally active, with paid rates of $2.27/mile now surpassing posted rates of $2.25/mile amidst a massive volume of over 35,800 available loads. This shift indicates that robust demand from construction and industrial projects is beginning to outpace the vast supply of available equipment, giving carriers a slight pricing advantage. While capacity is not yet scarce, the trend suggests the market is tightening. Brokers can still capitalize on the high volume, but should expect to pay a premium over posted rates to secure reliable capacity.
Diesel Price $3.73/gal - The national average diesel price has ticked up slightly to $3.73/gallon. This minor increase adds to carrier operating costs and reinforces their need for strong line-haul rates, reducing their flexibility on fuel surcharge negotiations. This trend supports overall rate firmness in the market.
OTRI Trends Tender rejection rates are expected to climb moderately this week, especially for van and reefer equipment in the Southeast. As the spread between contract and spot rates widens in key regions, carriers will increasingly reject tendered loads in favor of more lucrative spot market opportunities. This will place more pressure on brokers to cover last-minute freight for shippers.
