Vans The van market remains firm, with real-time paid rates averaging $1.88/mile, slightly ahead of the $1.86/mile average posted rate. Reefers The reefer market is experiencing a significant surge in rate pressure, creating critical challenges and opportunities. Real-time paid rates have jumped to an average of $2.34/mile, a substantial $0.12 premium over the average posted rate of $2.22/mile. This wide spread is a clear indicator of a severely constrained market where demand for temperature-controlled equipment, especially in produce-heavy regions like the Southeast, is far outstripping available capacity. With only 7,000 loads posted, the high paid rate suggests intense competition for a limited pool of trucks, forcing brokers to be aggressive in both pricing and sourcing to meet urgent shipper needs.
Flatbeds The flatbed segment continues to show robust strength and high volume, with paid rates at $2.31/mile surpassing posted rates of $2.27/mile. This indicates strong demand from construction and industrial sectors is absorbing available capacity, giving carriers leverage in negotiations. Despite a high volume of over 29,000 available loads, equipment is being booked quickly, preventing any rate softening. This market is less volatile than reefer but requires brokers to act decisively, as the favorable rate environment for carriers means equipment will not sit idle for long.
Diesel Price $3.73/gal - The national average for diesel has ticked up to $3.73/gallon. This increase, while minor, adds to carrier operating costs and reinforces the justification for current fuel surcharge levels. It's a key talking point for explaining total freight costs to shippers.
OTRI Trends Tender rejections are rising, particularly for reefer equipment. The significant premium on spot market paid rates is incentivizing carriers to reject contract loads in favor of more lucrative, immediate opportunities. Van rejections are also firming in high-demand regions. This trend puts pressure on brokers to offer competitive rates to secure capacity and fulfill commitments.
