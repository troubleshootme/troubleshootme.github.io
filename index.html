    • approves paperwork quickly
    • communicates clearly
    • solves fuel and facility questions early
    • will often win the truck even without being the very top rate.

⚠️ Margin traps and compliance risks

  • Underpriced reefer

    • $2.58 paid versus $2.52 posted means the screen is already behind the real truck.
    • If the lane is time-sensitive or storm-adjacent, the replacement cost can move against you quickly.

  • Flatbed sold without full jobsite detail

    • With $2.73 paid versus $2.62 posted, your risk is not just rate.
    • It is:
    • tarp exposure
    • securement time
    • crane delay
    • inaccessible site conditions
    • receiver delay

  • Heavy Haul quoted before permits and weather are checked

    • Severe winds make permit execution and safe routing part of the price.
    • Do not let sales outrun operations.

  • Van freight treated as uniformly soft

    • $2.08 paid against $2.17 posted gives some leverage.
    • But van can still turn expensive fast when:
    • the route is weather-hit
    • the delivery is rigid
    • the backhaul is weak
    • A negative spread is not a license to get careless.

  • Hours of Service (HOS) and adverse driving conditions

    • Weather detours and shutdowns can push drivers into compliance pressure.
    • Document everything:
    • weather notices
    • appointment changes
    • customer approvals
    • delay communications
    • That protects both service relationships and claims posture.

  • Carrier vetting still matters more in volatile markets

    • When the market gets stressful, bad actors surface.
    • Keep live driver verification, Electronic Logging Device (ELD) checks, and operating authority discipline intact, especially on:
    • weather-affected lanes
    • high-value freight
    • heavy haul
    • food-grade loads
