üìä Top-Line Summary

The national freight market continues in a holding pattern, characterized by overall stable load volumes but significant regional volatility driven by severe weather. Widespread flooding across the Southeast is causing major operational disruptions, tightening capacity and creating urgent, high-margin spot opportunities. Concurrently, an intense heatwave in the Southwest continues to constrain reefer availability and drive up rates. A key indicator of shifting market power is the persistent trend of paid rates exceeding posted rates for both van and reefer equipment, signaling that carriers hold negotiating leverage in these critical segments. Brokers must focus on navigating the weather-disrupted regions to capitalize on rate premiums while managing customer expectations regarding delays and increased costs.

üìà National Data Dashboard

Vans The van market is exhibiting clear signs of tightening, as the average paid rate of $1.87/mile now exceeds the average posted rate of $1.81/mile. This inversion, despite relatively stable load counts with 21,849 available loads, indicates that capacity is being absorbed and carriers are successfully rejecting initial offers, forcing brokers to increase bids to secure trucks. This dynamic suggests a reduction in carrier pliability, particularly in weather-affected regions, requiring more aggressive rate strategies to cover loads.
Reefers Reefer remains the most constrained and volatile segment, with a significant spread between the average paid rate ($2.38/mile) and the posted rate ($2.27/mile). With only 10,313 loads available, this premium is fueled by dual weather crises: extreme heat in the Southwest stressing equipment and drivers, and flooding in the Southeast disrupting produce shipments. This environment gives reefer carriers considerable pricing power, making it essential for brokers to quote at premium levels to secure scarce capacity for temperature-sensitive freight.
Flatbeds The flatbed market appears balanced and opportunity-rich, with a high volume of available loads (39,180) and identical average posted and paid rates of $2.23/mile. This equilibrium suggests that the broker-favorable rate spreads seen previously have normalized. While capacity is not overtly tight on a national level, regional disruptions, particularly from flooding impacting construction and industrial sites in the Southeast, can create localized sourcing challenges. Brokers can find ample opportunity here, but must be mindful of regional operating conditions.
Diesel Price $3.738/gal - The national average diesel price has ticked up slightly to $3.738/gallon. This minor increase adds to carrier operating costs and reinforces margin pressure, making precise fuel surcharge calculations critical for maintaining profitability on every load.
OTRI Trends Tender rejections are highest and climbing for all equipment types in the Southeast (GA, AL) due to widespread flooding, which creates hazardous driving conditions and operational shutdowns. Expect continued high rejection rates for reefer loads out of the Southwest (AZ, CA) as extreme heat makes carriers selective. This tightening capacity in key regions gives carriers leverage to reject undesirable freight.
