Vans The van market is exhibiting a bullish short-term trend as real-time paid rates have climbed to $1.89/mile, surpassing the average posted rate of $1.85/mile. Brokers must be prepared to pay market price for reliable reefer capacity, as carriers have ample opportunities and are less likely to discount rates for repositioning.

This dynamic is fueled by a substantial volume of over 34,000 available loads, driven by ongoing industrial manufacturing and construction projects. Although rates have softened from the peaks of early summer, the current positive spread between paid and posted rates indicates that demand is robust enough to support firm pricing. Capacity is generally available but is being quickly utilized, requiring brokers to act decisively to cover loads at competitive rates.

This persistent cost pressure is squeezing carrier margins, making them more selective about freight and less willing to accept low-paying loads. OTRI Trends Tender rejections are beginning to trend upward, especially in the Southeast and Midwest. As the spread between contract and spot rates narrows, and with paid spot rates exceeding posted rates, carriers are increasingly rejecting lower-paying contract freight. This trend will likely accelerate if spot rates continue to firm, tightening capacity for shippers relying on contracted carriers.
